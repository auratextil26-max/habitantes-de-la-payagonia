THE GUARDIAN OF THE STEPPE

A silent presence that sustains Patagonia's natural balance..

The puma is Patagonia's largest wild cat and one of South America's most emblematic predators. Silent, agile and adaptable, it inhabits mountains, steppes and forests..

During their first months of life, the cubs remain with their mother, learning to observe, move through the landscape, hunt and recognize their territory..

01

Large wild cat

It is the largest terrestrial carnivore found in Chile.

02

Wide distribution

It inhabits steppes, forests, mountains and shrublands.

03

Spotted cubs

Puma cubs are born with spots that disappear as they grow.

04

Solitary life

It is territorial and generally solitary.

CONSERVATION

Protecting the puma means preserving Patagonia's natural balance.

Habitat loss and human persecution are among the threats it faces. Conserving the landscapes where it lives also helps protect many other Patagonian species.
